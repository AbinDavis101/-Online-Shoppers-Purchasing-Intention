The research examines how to use machine learning and explainable artificial intelligence (XAI) to forecast the purchase intention of online shoppers based on the session-level behavioural data. The study is designed to counter one of the main weaknesses in current e-commerce analytics; many models tend to focus on predictive accuracy and fail to present insights that can be interpreted and used in making decision.
A structured way of doing things was adopted with the use of the Online Shoppers Purchasing Intention dataset, involving data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, model development, and evaluation. Several classification models that included: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Support Vector Machine and XGBoost were compared. The performance shows that ensemble models, especially, XGBoost and Random Forest, perform best in terms of predictive power, with the ability to predict complex and nonlinear behavioural trends.
